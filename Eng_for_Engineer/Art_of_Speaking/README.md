# **Improved & Clear Explanation for Better English Communication**

When we communicate, our **first and most important goal** is to build a relationship with the other person.
If we fail to create a relaxed and comfortable connection, the rest of the conversation becomes harder.

So, in any conversation, our first task is to build **rapport**.


## **What is Rapport?**

- Rappo0rt is the feeling that the other person is ‚Äúlike us‚Äù ‚Äî that we connect with them and feel comfortable.
- It happens when there is **similarity in communication style**.
Rapport is basically a pattern-matching process.

Interestingly, **most rapport-building happens without words**.

We build rapport through:

* Body orientation
* How we move
* Eye contact
* Facial expressions
* Tone of voice

Humans do this naturally ‚Äî it‚Äôs instinctive.
However, stress, workplace pressure, or emotional tension can weaken these natural patterns.
A little **conscious effort** at the start of a conversation can greatly improve how it goes.



## **How We Build Rapport**

We build rapport through three kinds of behavior:

1. **Verbal behavior** ‚Äì the words we use
2. **Vocal behavior** ‚Äì tone, pitch, pace, volume
3. **Physical behavior** ‚Äì body language, posture, expression

### **1. Verbal Behavior (Words)**

Words do matter.
For example:

* ‚ùå ‚ÄúThat is sad.‚Äù
* ‚úÖ ‚ÄúOh, it‚Äôs disheartening to hear that. I‚Äôm sure it must be stressful for you.‚Äù

Both convey sympathy, but the second one shows **deeper empathy**.


### **2. Physical Behavior (Body Language)**

If words and body language do not match, people believe the **body**, not the words.

Example:

* If you greet someone with no smile and a cold expression but say,
  ‚ÄúIt‚Äôs such a pleasure to see you,‚Äù
  they will believe your **face**, not your words.

So, to build rapport, show physical signs of being:

* Welcoming
* Relaxed
* Open


### **3. Vocal Behavior (Tone of Voice)**

Tone changes meaning.

Compare:

* ‚ÄúI‚Äôm really glad to see you.‚Äù (warm)
* ‚ÄúI‚Äôm really glad to see you.‚Äù (flat or irritated tone)

Pitch, pace, and volume matter:

* **Fast, loud, high-pitched ‚Üí** you sound tense or nervous
* **Slow, soft, lower-pitched ‚Üí** you sound calm and friendly

A warm tone builds rapport faster.

## **Small Talk Helps Build Rapport**

Starting a conversation with someone new can feel stressful or awkward.
‚ÄúBreaking the ice‚Äù is a skill, and the key is to **reduce tension**.

How to start?

* Talk about something you both share in the moment
* Make a simple, friendly comment
* Ask a question that doesn‚Äôt feel personal or intrusive

Avoid:

* Talking too much about yourself
* Asking direct personal questions
  Both can increase tension.

Instead, make them feel comfortable.
When they relax, **you** will relax too.

Example at a networking event:

> ‚ÄúThe event management seems really good this time, doesn‚Äôt it?‚Äù

This simple comment opens the door to a conversation.
What happens next depends on how you continue with engaging statements and supportive responses.


# **In Summary**

To improve communication and build rapport:

1. Match body language and tone naturally
2. Use empathetic and thoughtful words
3. Keep your tone warm and calm
4. Start conversations with simple, shared-situation comments
5. Focus on making the other person comfortable

When rapport is established early, conversations become smoother, easier, and more meaningful.

## **What is ‚ÄúI-Talk‚Äù?**

**I-Talk** means expressing your feelings and concerns by starting with **‚ÄúI‚Äù** instead of blaming the other person with **‚ÄúYou‚Äù**.

### Why it works:

* No one can argue with your **feelings**.
* You avoid **blame**, so the other person is less defensive.
* You focus on the **impact**, not the person‚Äôs character.
* It invites a **calm conversation** instead of a fight.

### Example:

‚ùå **Accusation (You-talk):**
‚ÄúYou never do the dishes. This house is always a mess because of you!‚Äù

‚úîÔ∏è **I-Talk (Better):**
‚ÄúI feel stressed and disappointed when I come home and see that the dishes are undone after you promised to do them.‚Äù

This changes the tone completely.


## **Why is I-Talk powerful?**

Because:

* People may argue with your *interpretation*
  (‚ÄúI didn‚Äôt do the dishes because I was busy!‚Äù)
* But they **cannot deny your feelings**
  (‚ÄúYou can‚Äôt feel disappointed!‚Äù)

It becomes about **your inner experience**, not their failure.


## **What is ‚ÄúWe-Talk‚Äù?**

‚ÄúWe-Talk‚Äù replaces ‚ÄúI‚Äù with **‚Äúwe‚Äù** when discussing shared actions, shared memories, or shared responsibilities.

### Why it works:

* Builds a sense of **teamwork**
* Shows **unity** and removes ‚Äúme vs. you‚Äù
* Creates emotional closeness
* Makes the other person feel included and important

### Example:

‚ùå ‚ÄúI think the meeting starts at 3.‚Äù
(This sounds individual.)

‚úîÔ∏è ‚ÄúWe need to be in the conference room by 3, right?‚Äù
(This sounds cooperative‚Äîsame meaning, but more connected.)

Another:

‚ùå ‚ÄúI remember the summer of 2012. It was really hot.‚Äù
‚úîÔ∏è ‚ÄúWe had a really hot summer in 2012.‚Äù
(Instantly feels like a shared memory.)


## **What exactly changes?**

The **words are small**, but the **impact is huge**.

### When you use **You-Talk**:

* The other person feels attacked
* They defend themselves
* The conversation becomes a fight

### When you use **I-Talk**:

* The other person understands how you feel
* They become less defensive
* They are more open to change

### When you use **We-Talk**:

* You create closeness
* You build teamwork
* You encourage cooperation


## Simple summary

* **I-Talk** = expressing *your* feelings ‚Üí calm conversation
* **We-Talk** = highlighting *togetherness* ‚Üí collaboration
* **You-Talk** = blame ‚Üí arguments & defensiveness.

## **Concept: Correct the Behavior, Not the Person**

When someone is not doing their work ‚Äî for example, spending too much time on social media or coming late ‚Äî the natural reaction is to label them:

* ‚ÄúYou are lazy.‚Äù
* ‚ÄúYou don‚Äôt care about your work.‚Äù
* ‚ÄúYou are irresponsible.‚Äù

But the moment you attack their **personality**, they become **defensive**.
Instead of listening, they start thinking of excuses.

To avoid this, focus only on:

### ‚úî **Facts**

### ‚úî **Observable actions**

### ‚úî **Consequences of those actions**

Not on their **character**.

This makes the person realize the issue without feeling insulted, and they become more cooperative.


## **How to Correct Behavior Without Blaming Personality**

### **1. Talk about the facts**

Mention what actually happened ‚Äî the specific behavior.

### **2. Explain why the behavior is a problem**

Connect it to project deadlines, team impact, or expectations.

### **3. State the consequences or expectations**

Tell them what needs to change going forward.

This reduces defensiveness and increases accountability.


## **Example: Employee wasting time on social media**

‚ùå **Don‚Äôt say:**
‚ÄúYou‚Äôre lazy and wasting time. Stop using social media!‚Äù

‚úî **Say instead:**
‚ÄúHey, I wanted to discuss something.
I noticed that in the past few days, you‚Äôve spent around **2‚Äì3 hours during work hours** on social media.
The project that was supposed to be completed on Monday is still pending.
Since this project is high priority, we need your complete focus on it.
Let‚Äôs try to keep personal browsing outside work hours so we can finish this on time.‚Äù

Here you are:

* stating **facts**
* showing **impact**
* requesting a **change**
* not attacking the person

This makes them realize the issue without feeling judged.


## **Example: Employee coming late**

‚ùå **Don‚Äôt say:**
‚ÄúYou don‚Äôt care about your job. You‚Äôre irresponsible.‚Äù

‚úî **Say:**
‚ÄúYou have come late **six times in the last two weeks**.

Again:

* Behavior ‚Üí mentioned
* Impact ‚Üí explained
* Consequence ‚Üí stated
* Personality attack ‚Üí avoided

Guide you voice

- The best way to improve how you speak is to **listen to your own voice**.

- When we talk to people, we cannot hear ourselves properly.

- When you listen to the recording, you will understand:

- This will show your real style ‚Äî your pauses, tone, pace, etc.

* pitch
* tone
* energy

- Make small changes slowly.
- It will not improve in one day, but with practice you will sound better and more confident.

- But if you keep correcting yourself and practicing, your speaking style will improve and any issues you had will slowly disappear.

# **Why Eye Contact is Important**

* In **everyday conversation**, we naturally look at the person we are talking to.
* In **public speaking**, this is even more important because it **keeps your audience engaged**.
* If you stare at the wall, ceiling, or a blank space, your speech loses energy and connection.

**Key idea:** Eye contact makes your audience **feel included and involved** in your talk.


# **How to Start With Eye Contact**

1. **Find someone familiar:**

   * If you know someone in the audience, start by talking to them.
   * This eases nervousness and makes the speech feel less intimidating.

2. **If you don‚Äôt know anyone:**

   * Pretend you are talking to a friend one-on-one.
   * This turns a public speech into a personal conversation, which is easier to handle.

3. **Hold your gaze:**

   * Lock eyes with one person for **10‚Äì15 seconds** (roughly one complete thought).
   * Don‚Äôt just glance and move on ‚Äî the audience feels more connected when you focus on them for a moment.


# **Practical Example (Using the Veterinary Clinic Speech)**

* Thought 1:

> ‚ÄúI interned at the veterinary clinic for a month, and the experience was enriching.‚Äù
> **Lock eyes with one person for this thought.**

* Thought 2:

> ‚ÄúI learned valuable skills that I can use in my future job.‚Äù
> **Switch to another person and maintain eye contact for this thought.**

‚ùå What **not to do**: Rapidly switch eyes multiple times in a single sentence.

* It **reduces impact** and makes your speech seem disconnected.


# **Tips to Reduce Nervousness**

* Take a deep breath before starting.
* Relax ‚Äî you don‚Äôt have to be overly serious.
* Some suggest imagining the audience in funny ways (like wearing underwear) to ease nerves ‚Äî but this is optional.
* Confidence in your **preparation and content** is usually enough.


# **Key Takeaways**

1. Eye contact = engagement + connection.
2. Start with someone familiar or pretend one-on-one conversation.
3. Hold gaze for 10‚Äì15 seconds per thought.
